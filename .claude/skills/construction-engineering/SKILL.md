# Construction Engineering Skill — LániCAD

## Product Systems & Suppliers

### Mobile Fences (Girðingar)
- **Supplier reference**: kkservice.pl (Polish manufacturer)
- **Types**: Standard industrial/construction temporary fences
- **Panels**: Welded steel mesh in galvanized tube frame
- **Dimensions**: 3500×2000mm (1.1 or 1.7 kg/m wire), 3500×1200mm, 2500mm queue barriers, 2100×1100mm plastic
- **Feet**: Concrete stones (35×22×15cm) or PVC bases
- **Connections**: Tube clamps join adjacent panels at top rail
- **Gates**: 120cm pedestrian gates with upper locking mechanism
- **Assembly rule**: Each panel needs 2 feet (one at each end), shared between adjacent panels. N panels need N+1 stones.

### Mobile Scaffolding — Hjólapallar (Frigerio Alupont)
- **Supplier**: Frigerio S.p.A. (Italy)
- **Systems**:
  - **0.75m (B1/B5)**: Alupont B74 — narrow tower, 0.75m wide, aluminum H-frames
  - **1.35m (F1/F5)**: Alupont F135 — wide tower, 1.35m wide, aluminum H-frames
  - **Quickly**: Alupont Quick — foldable scaffold, single-person setup
- **Structure**: 
  - Aluminum H-frames (B1=2.1m, B5=1.05m tall) stacked vertically
  - Pin-connected frame joints
  - Diagonal braces on long sides (alternating X-pattern)
  - Platform boards at working height (aluminum deck with trap door for ladder access)
  - Castors (Ø200mm) with brakes and adjustable legs
  - Outriggers/stabilizers required above certain heights
- **Height range**: 2.5m to 10.5m platform height in 1.0m increments
- **Safety**: Full guardrails (top + mid) on all four sides, toeboard at platform level
- **Key dimensions**:
  - Frame length: ~2.5m (along working direction)
  - Narrow width: 0.75m, Wide width: 1.35m
  - Guardrail height: 1.0m above platform
  - Castors add ~0.25m to base height

### Facade Scaffolding — Vinnupallar (Layher)
- **Systems**:
  - **Layher Allround (AGS)**: Rosette-node modular scaffolding — steel/aluminum, 48.3mm tube diameter
  - **Layher SpeedyScaf**: Quick-erect frame scaffolding
- **Layher Allround dimensions**:
  - Standard bay length: 2.57m (also 1.09m, 1.40m, 1.57m, 2.07m, 3.07m available)
  - Bay depth (width): 0.73m standard, 1.09m wide
  - Vertical frame height: 2.0m (standard) or 0.7m (half-height)
  - Rosette spacing: 0.5m vertical intervals on standards
- **Components per bay**:
  - 2× vertical standards (front + back)
  - 2× ledgers (horizontal, front + back)
  - 1× transverse connector (cross-brace between front/back)
  - 1× platform (steel/aluminum deck board)
  - 1× diagonal brace (alternating pattern)
  - Wall ties: 1 per 15m² of scaffold face
- **Legs/Screw jacks**: 50cm or 100cm adjustable base jacks
- **Safety**: Double guardrails (top 1.0m + mid 0.5m), toeboard 15cm, end-stops
- **Roof systems**:
  - **Keder Roof XL**: Weather protection, keder rail + membrane
  - **Allround FW Roof**: Truss-type roof for wider spans
- **Weight calculation**: Sum all component weights × quantities

### Ceiling Props — Loftastoðir (Villalta)
- **Supplier**: Villalta S.r.l. (Italy)
- **Type**: Telescopic acrow/steel props (EN 1065 certified)
- **Classes** (EN 1065):
  - A: 0.7–2.0m range, 20–52.1 kN capacity
  - B/BD: 1.6–2.9m range, 20–35 kN
  - CD: 2.0–3.5m range, 15–35 kN
  - CE: 2.5–4.0m range, 12–30 kN
  - D/E: 3.0–5.5m range, 10–30 kN
- **Structure**:
  - Outer tube (bottom): larger diameter, thicker wall
  - Inner tube (top): telescopic, slides inside outer tube
  - Adjustment collar with pins at fixed intervals
  - Fine adjustment via threaded spindle/handle
  - Base plate: 150×150mm steel plate
  - Head plate: 120×120mm (supports beam or slab directly)
- **HT-20 Beams**: Timber/metal I-beams spanning between prop heads
  - Lengths: 2.45m, 2.90m, 3.30m, 3.90m, 4.90m (HT-20), 3.60m, 5.90m (Faresin)
  - Cross-section: ~80mm wide × 200mm deep
  - Placed perpendicular to secondary beams or directly supporting plywood
- **Layout rules**:
  - Props in grid pattern, spacing depends on slab thickness and load
  - Typical: 1.0–1.5m grid for normal 20cm slabs
  - Beams span between prop heads, plywood on top of beams
  - Tripod base available for stability on uneven ground

### Wall Formwork — Hünnebeck Systems

#### Panel Systems
- **Rasto**: Light hand-set wall formwork
  - Panel sizes: 30–240cm wide × 150 or 300cm tall
  - Steel frame with plywood face (18mm birch ply)
  - Weight: ~40–80 kg/m² depending on size
  - **MP panels**: Multi-purpose panels with movable cross-members
  - Corner elements: Inside corners, outside corners (Veltihorn), adjustable
  - Compensation fillers (Hornastillar): 5cm wide strips

- **Rasto G2**: Updated generation — improved clamp connections

- **Manto**: Heavy-duty crane-handled wall formwork
  - Panel sizes: 45–240cm wide × 330cm tall
  - Steel frame, heavier than Rasto, designed for crane handling
  - Higher concrete pressures (up to 80 kN/m²)
  - Manto G3/G3M: Latest generation with improved alignment

- **Platinum**: Premium system — highest finish quality

#### CRITICAL: Two-Sided Formwork Rule
**Walls ALWAYS require formwork on BOTH sides.** For every meter of wall:
- 2× panel sets (one each side)
- Tie rods through the wall connecting both sides
- Cone spacers to maintain wall thickness
- The calculation must always double the panel quantity

#### Tie Rods & Spacing
- **DW15 tie rods**: Standard 15mm diameter, threaded
- Spacing depends on concrete pressure:
  - Normal walls (≤3m high, 40 kN/m²): ~60cm horizontal × 60cm vertical
  - Tall walls (>3m, higher pressure): ~45cm × 50cm
- **Rule of thumb**: ~3 tie rods per m² of wall face
- Each tie rod needs: 1 rod + 2 wing nuts + 2 cones + 2 washers
- Cone spacers set the wall thickness (15, 20, 25, 30cm typical)

#### Aligning Struts (Push-Pull Props)
- Angled supports holding formwork vertical
- Typically at 30° angle from wall
- Spacing: ~1.2m along wall length
- Anchor to ground slab or weighted base
- One strut per panel width is standard

#### Support Frames
- Used instead of struts for free-standing walls
- Triangular bracing frames
- One per panel width, both sides

### Slab Formwork — TOPEC (Hünnebeck)
- **System**: Drop-head formwork for concrete slabs
- **Components**:
  - TOPEC panels (aluminum frames with plywood face)
  - Drop-heads allow early stripping while props remain
  - Primary beams (HT-20 timber I-beams)
  - Secondary beams perpendicular to primary
  - Steel props supporting the system from below
  - TOP START: Starting configuration kit
  - TOPEC Wall Flange: Edge connection to walls
  - Racing Frame: Speed assembly frame system
  - Universal Prop: Adjustable steel prop
- **Assembly**:
  1. Set props in grid pattern
  2. Place primary beams on prop heads
  3. Place secondary beams perpendicular on primaries
  4. Lay TOPEC panels on secondaries
  5. Fine-level and pour concrete
- **Panel faces**: Plywood (standard) or Ecoply (newer, reusable plastic-faced)

### Shoring Systems (Hünnebeck)
- **ST-60**: Heavy-duty shoring tower
  - Modular frame tower system
  - For high loads and tall clearances
  - Crosses and ledgers form rigid 3D frame
- **ID-15**: Modular steel shoring system
  - Lighter than ST-60, faster assembly
  - Used for bridge decks, elevated slabs

### Safety Systems
- **Protecto G2**: Edge protection and safety barrier system
  - Post + rail system
  - Clamps to slab edges
  - EN 13374 compliant

## Fastener/Screw Rules by Formwork Generation

### Rasto (G1)
- Panel-to-panel: 2× Schnellspanner (quick clamps) per vertical joint
- Corner connections: 2× clamps per corner
- Tie rod at every grid intersection (~60cm spacing)
- Strut connection: 1× bracket per strut

### Rasto G2
- Improved quick-release clamps
- Same spacing rules as G1 but fewer clamps needed (larger engagement area)
- Panel-to-panel: 2× G2 clamps per joint (top + bottom)

### Manto / Manto G3
- BFD clamps (Butterfly Fastener): 2× per vertical joint for standard panels
- 3× BFD clamps for panels > 2.0m tall
- Corner clamps: Specialized Manto corner clamp system
- Tie rods: DW20 (20mm) for Manto, higher load capacity

### General Screw/Bolt Counts
- **Per standard wall panel** (e.g., 90×300 Rasto):
  - 2 quick clamps to join to neighbor
  - ~5 tie rods through the panel (grid at 60cm)
  - 1 strut bracket
- **Per Manto panel** (e.g., 90×330):
  - 2–3 BFD clamps
  - ~6 tie rods (higher panel, more rows)
  - 1 strut connection

## Concrete Pressure Calculation
- Fresh concrete pressure: P = γ × h × C
  - γ = unit weight of concrete ≈ 25 kN/m³
  - h = height of fluid concrete (max = pour height if rate < 1m/hr)
  - C = coefficient for pour rate and temperature
- Max pressure for Rasto: 40 kN/m² (4m head)
- Max pressure for Manto: 80 kN/m² (crane-set, higher-rated)
- Tie rod spacing must keep actual stress below allowable

## 3D Visualization Standards for LániCAD

### Tube/Pipe Rendering
- Standard scaffold tube: 48.3mm OD → radius 0.024m
- Thin braces/rails: ~32mm OD → radius 0.016m
- Fence tube frame: ~42mm OD → radius 0.021m
- Use `<cylinderGeometry>` with quaternion rotation for arbitrary orientation
- Metallic appearance: metalness 0.6–0.7, roughness 0.3

### Color Coding
- Steel tubes: #777 to #888
- Aluminum tubes: #aaa to #bbb
- Guardrails: #cc0000 (red for safety)
- Platform/deck boards: #f5c800 (brand yellow)
- Concrete/base: #999 to #bbb
- Ground plane: #e0e0e0
- Formwork panels: 
  - Rasto: #c06030 (orange-brown, representing plywood/steel)
  - Manto: #4a6fa5 (blue-grey steel)
  - TOPEC: #7a8a6a (green-grey aluminum)
- Props: Inner tube #aaa, outer tube #888
- Plywood face: #c8a870 (wood color)

### Mesh Infill for Fences
- Use wireframe plane for mesh panels (not solid — fences are see-through)
- Horizontal wire spacing: ~50mm
- Vertical wire spacing: ~200mm
- Actual mesh is welded wire grid in galvanized steel

### Formwork Assembly Visualization
- Show both sides of wall with gap between (wall thickness)
- Tie rods visible through the gap
- Struts angled at ~30° from wall
- Panel edges outlined in dark (#333) for distinction
